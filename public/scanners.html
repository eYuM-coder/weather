    How it works

    • YallBot is listening to the scanners: We continuously collect the latest radio traffic from public emergency scanner feeds across many counties.
    • We bundle the latest: We keep a rolling snapshot of what just came across the air and which areas are active right now.
    • YallBot reads it all: We feed that live picture to YallBot, our weather-savvy AI helper focused on storm impacts and emergency response.
    • It writes the story: YallBot returns a structured summary that includes:
    • Overall summary: A short, human-readable narrative of what's happening
    • Key incidents: The most important weather-related events (e.g., flooding, wind damage, tornado activity)
    • County snapshots: Activity leavels, warnings, and notable transmissions by county
    • We save it for the site: The summary is stored behind the scenes and the website updates automatically.

    What we focus on

    • > Included: Weather damage, flooding, tornado/wind/hail reports, weather-related rescues, power outages, road closures due to weather.
    • > Excluded: Routine medical or police calls that aren't driven by weather.

    If it’s quiet

    • > If the scanners are quiet, you’ll see a calm update letting you know there’s nothing major at the moment.

    Where the info comes from

    • > Public scanner feeds: We only use publicly available emergency radio streams.
    • > Automated system: No private data, no personal details—just public radio traffic and weather content.

    Why it's helpful

    Fast situational awareness: One place to see the important weather-related things happening across many scanner channels.

    Clear and current: Easy to understand, refreshed regularly as new transmissions roll in.

    Why this is different

    Real-time superpower: YallBot listens to dozens of channels at once, then distills the noise into a clean briefing.

    Weather-first lens: It spots weather damage, flooding, and storm patterns across counties—fast.

    From chatter to clarity: Instead of scrolling raw radio logs, you get a clear picture of what matters now.

    Made for the public: No jargon. Just what's happeninng, where, and why it matters.

    Important note

    • ! YallBot can make mistakes: It can mishear radio audio or misunderstand a transmission. Treat this as helpful guidance—not official emergency information. Always confirm with local authorities and official alerts when making safety decisions.
