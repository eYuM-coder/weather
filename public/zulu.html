    UTC a.k.a. Zulu

    Zulu time is simply Coordinated Universal Time (UTC). The letter "Z" (pronounced "Zulu" in the phonetic alphabet) is used in timestamps to indicate UTC, for example 18:30Z. It never observes Daylight Saving Time.

    EXAMPLE: 8:00 PM ET during Standard Time is 01:00Z the next day; during Daylight Time it is 00:00Z. This is why the exact date matters when converting times.

    Why Weather Uses Zulu

    • > Global coordination across time zones
    • > No ambiguity with Daylight Saving changes
    • > Consistent timestamps on model runs, radar, and satellite products
    • > Aviation, marine, and emergency management standards

    Reading Zulu Timestamps

    A timestamp like 2025-02-03 12:00Z means 12:00 UTC on Feb 3, 2025. Subtract or add your local offset (considering DST) to get local time, or use the converter above.

    Quick Reference (approximate)

    ET: Standard UTC-5, Daylight UTC-4
    CT: Standard UTC-6, Daylight UTC-5
    MT: Standard UTC-7, Daylight UTC-6

    AKT: Standard UTC-9, Daylight UTC-8
    HST: UTC-10 (no DST)

    Operational Data

    • > Weather models run at 00Z, 06Z, 12Z, and 18Z, 24/7/365.
